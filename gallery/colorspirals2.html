Color Spirals | Series 002
— 2013 —

Another year's worth of swirls, most created around the start of the year. Bright colors, daring loops, our journey pushes us further into the depths into warping spirals. These were largely produced in the same tools as their predescessors, namely python. We think it's best described as hitting them with a forming-hammer shaped block of code.

We've enjoyed our time sending pens sweeping back and forth accros the page, reveled in the subtle differences we experience each trip through the current function, and rejoiced in the brief moments our warped, reticulating line crossed itself once again. If we could relax, this would be pleasure.

These were designed using Inkscape + Python,
and executed by a modified Mimaki CG-45 Vinyl Cutter

This set of spiral forms was drawn with Black Sharpies
and a wide selection of Moonglow and Metallic Sakura Gelly-Roll Pens
on 80lb Cardstock.
