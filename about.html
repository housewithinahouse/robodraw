Mimaki CG-45
Vinyl Cutter

The Mimaki CG-45 is a 1989 vinyl cutter. It came to us from a garage sale (dusty, and in need of some love). We fixed it up, and now it's happily drawing again.

Although originally intended to cut vinyl for signs, this machine has been converted into a pen plotter. This is a robot that draws.

The machine is capable of 3 different types of movements. For the X axis, there is a carriage that moves back and forth. The pen rides on this; sensing electronics live here. For the Y axis movement, there is a friction roller that moves the paper rather than the pen. The third movement is via a pen lift solenoid. This removes the pen from the paper so you can do more than draw a continuous line.

These three movements make up the whole of the machine's capabilities, from which one may make a huge variety of drawings. All of the drawings for the Mimaki have, so far, been composed in Inkscape, a free vector graphics program.
